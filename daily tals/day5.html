Y. S. Rajasekhara Reddy

Yeduguri Sandinti Rajasekhara Reddy8 July 1949 – 2 September 2009, popularly known as YSR, was an Indian politician. He served as the 14th chief minister of Andhra Pradesh from 2004 to 2009. Reddy was elected (1989, 1991, 1996 and 1998) four terms to the Lok Sabha from Kadapa. He was also elected (1978, 1983, 1985, 1999, 2004 and 2009) six terms to the Andhra Pradesh Legislative Assembly from Pulivendula. Over the course of his career, he won every election that he contested, either to Assembly or Lok Sabha.

Padayatra

In 2003, he undertook a three-month-long padayatra covering 1,500 kilometres (930 mi) in 60 days across 11 districts of Andhra Pradesh as a part of his election campaign. He led the Congress party to victory in the 2004 and 2009 assembly elections

YSR was a member of the Indian National Congress party, which he led to decisive victories in the state assembly elections of 2004 and 2009. His tenure as Chief Minister was marked by several welfare schemes, including the Aarogyasri health insurance scheme and the free electricity scheme for farmers. He was known for his charismatic leadership and mass appeal, particularly among rural voters.

Early and personal life

Y. S. Rajasekhara Reddy was born on 8 July 1949 in a small village in Andhra Pradesh. He was the son of a local farmer and showed an early interest in politics and social issues.

He pursued his education in Hyderabad and later became involved in student politics. His early experiences in rural Andhra Pradesh shaped his understanding of the needs and aspirations of the common people.

Death

On 2 September 2009, Y. S. Rajasekhara Reddy died in a helicopter crash in the Nallamala Hills near Rudravaram, Krishna district, Andhra Pradesh. The crash occurred during inclement weather while he was on an official visit to the region. His death was a significant loss to the state and led to widespread mourning across Andhra Pradesh.

The circumstances surrounding his death led to various investigations and speculations, but it was officially concluded that the crash was an accident. His legacy continues through his family members who remain active in politics.

Legacy

YSR's tenure as Chief Minister left a lasting impact on Andhra Pradesh. His welfare schemes and development initiatives are remembered fondly by many residents of the state. He is often regarded as a leader who prioritized the needs of the common people and worked towards their betterment.

His son, Y. S. Jaganmohan Reddy, continues to be an influential political figure in Andhra Pradesh, carrying forward his father's vision and policies.

Conclusion

Y. S. Rajasekhara Reddy remains a significant figure in the political history of Andhra Pradesh. His contributions to the state's development and his dedication to public service have left an enduring legacy.

Despite his untimely death, YSR's influence continues through his family and the policies he implemented during his tenure as Chief Minister.

Andhra PCC Chairman (1983-85)

Reddy served as the President of the Andhra Pradesh Congress Committee (APCC) for the first time from 1983 to 1985. He was appointed to this position by Indira Gandhi at the age of 34, following the Congress party's significant loss in Andhra Pradesh to the Telugu Desam Party in 1983. As state party president, he led the Congress party into the 1985 Assembly elections but was unable to overcome the popularity of the TDP led by N.T. Rama Rao

Chief Ministership (2004–09)

Reddy was elected in the 2004 Andhra Pradesh state assembly elections, and he was sworn in as the 14th Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh on 14 May 2004. He won a landslide victory, securing 185 seats out of 294 in the assembly. His tenure was marked by several welfare schemes and development initiatives aimed at improving the lives of the people in the state.

Aarogyasri Scheme

The Aarogyasri Scheme is a health insurance scheme launched by the Government of Andhra Pradesh to provide financial protection to families against catastrophic medical expenses.

The scheme covers a wide range of medical procedures and treatments, ensuring that families do not face financial hardship due to health-related issues. It has been praised for its impact on improving access to healthcare for the underprivileged sections of society.

Apart from the last three 5 year plans, which advocated PPP in health-care, other factors led to starting of Aarogyasri in AP. Jayati Ghosh committee[26] on the farmer's suicide in AP brought out the precarious health conditions due to economic distress in the agriculture sector and suggested free care of the poor by the private hospitals, which have benefitted from state subsidies. These recommendations of the report became convenient support to him, being a medical doctor himself, to provide this innovative health-care scheme for all, through corporate hospitals. Earlier CM relief fund had spent Rs. 168.52 crores to help 55,362 below the poverty line (BPL) patients needing hospitalization. Simultaneously, Dalit movement highlighted problems of young children with heart ailments in the state. Soon, He announced free heart surgeries for these children, by August 2006, 4600 children were operated under the CM relief fund. This magnanimous gesture set the stage for Aarogyasri scheme.

Second term as the Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh (2009)

He led the congress party to victory in the 2009 assembly election, winning 156 seats in the assembly. He became the first incumbent chief minister from Congress since 1969 (after Kasu Brahmananda Reddy in 1969) to win and was sworn in as the 15th Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh on 20 May 2009. He termed the results the "people's victory" and vowed to continue welfare and development schemes, especially construction of irrigation project'.

Reddy led the congress party to victory in the 2004 assembly election and served as the 14th Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh from 2004 to 2009. During his tenure as Chief Minister, he enacted schemes to provide free electricity for farmers, a health insurance program for rural people living below the poverty line,[20] a free public ambulance service,[21] low interest loans to rural women, subsidised housing for the rural poor,[22] subsidized rice,[23] reimbursement of college fees for underprivileged and reservation for minorities.[24] His tenure also saw the weakening of the violent extremist left-wing Naxalite movement that was rampant in the state.[11] He commenced the Jala Yagnam project to irrigate 4,000,000 hectares (10,000,000 acres) of land through the construction of major, medium and minor irrigation projects
